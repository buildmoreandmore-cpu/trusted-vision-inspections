General Questions

What is a home inspection?

A home inspection is a thorough, non-invasive visual examination of a property's major systems and components. At Trusted Vision, Carl personally walks through every property using a detailed 400+ point checklist covering the roof, foundation, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, interior, exterior, and more. You'll receive a comprehensive report with photos documenting the condition of everything we evaluate. Learn more about our inspection services.

How long does a home inspection take?

A standard home inspection typically takes 2 to 4 hours depending on the size, age, and condition of the property. A 1,500 sq ft condo may take around 2 hours, while a 4,000 sq ft home with a crawl space and detached structures can take 4 hours or more. Carl never rushes an inspection -- thoroughness is the whole point.

Should I attend the inspection?

Absolutely. While it's not required, we strongly encourage you to attend. Carl walks you through the property at the end of every inspection, pointing out issues in person and explaining what matters, what's minor, and what you should negotiate on. It's one of the most valuable parts of the process. If you can't attend, you'll still receive a detailed photo-documented report the same day.

When will I receive my inspection report?

You'll receive your full inspection report the same day as your inspection, typically within a few hours of completion. The report includes detailed descriptions, photographs, and recommendations for every finding. Check out our sample report page to see exactly what's included.

Georgia-Specific Questions

Is a home inspection required in Georgia?

Georgia does not legally require a home inspection before purchase, but it is strongly recommended. Under the Georgia Association of Realtors (GAR) contract, buyers have a due diligence period (typically 7-14 days) to conduct inspections and negotiate repairs. Skipping the inspection means you could inherit costly problems without recourse. Read our Georgia Due Diligence guide for more details.

What is the Georgia due diligence period?

The due diligence period is a negotiated timeframe (usually 7-14 days) written into the GAR purchase contract. During this period, you can:

  • Conduct a home inspection and any additional testing
  • Request repairs or credits based on findings
  • Walk away from the deal for any reason (though you may forfeit your due diligence fee)

We recommend scheduling your inspection as soon as your offer is accepted to maximize your time within this window.

Are home inspectors licensed in Georgia?

Yes. Georgia requires all home inspectors to be licensed through the Georgia Secretary of State. Carl Hall is a Georgia-licensed home inspector, ASHI Certified, and an InterNACHI member. These credentials mean he follows the highest standards of practice in the industry, going beyond Georgia's minimum requirements.

What does Georgia law require an inspector to cover?

Georgia Standards of Practice require inspectors to evaluate the structural components, exterior, roofing, plumbing, electrical, heating, air conditioning, insulation, ventilation, and interior of a home. At Trusted Vision, we go well beyond the state minimum with our 400+ point checklist that also covers grading and drainage, appliance function, safety hazards, and more. View our full report details to see everything we cover.

Our Services

Do you offer radon testing?

Yes. Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas that can seep into homes through the foundation. It's the #2 leading cause of lung cancer in the U.S. Parts of Metro Atlanta, especially areas in North Fulton, Gwinnett, and DeKalb counties, have elevated radon levels. Our radon testing service uses continuous monitoring devices placed for 48+ hours to get an accurate reading. Add radon testing to any inspection for $200.

When should I get mold testing?

We recommend mold testing if you notice musty odors, visible discoloration, recent water damage, or if the home has a crawl space (common in older Atlanta homes). Atlanta's hot, humid climate makes mold growth more likely, especially in basements and crawl spaces. Our mold testing includes air quality sampling and surface testing sent to an accredited laboratory. Add it to any inspection for $350.

Do I need lead paint testing?

If the home was built before 1978, lead paint testing is highly recommended, especially if you have young children. Many of Atlanta's historic neighborhoods -- Virginia-Highland, Grant Park, Inman Park, and East Atlanta -- have beautiful older homes that may contain lead-based paint. Our testing uses XRF analyzers and lab analysis to identify lead hazards. Available for $150 as an add-on.

You save over $200 compared to booking each service individually. It's our most popular package and the best value, especially for older homes or first-time buyers who want complete peace of mind.

Atlanta-Specific Questions

What areas of Metro Atlanta do you serve?

We serve all of Metro Atlanta including Atlanta, Alpharetta, Buckhead, Decatur, Dunwoody, East Point, Kennesaw, Lawrenceville, Marietta, Peachtree City, Roswell, Sandy Springs, Smyrna, Stockbridge, Stone Mountain, Woodstock, and surrounding communities. If your property is within the Metro Atlanta area, we can inspect it. Contact us if you're unsure whether your location is in our service area.

What common issues do you find in Atlanta homes?

Metro Atlanta homes commonly have issues related to our climate and soil conditions:

  • Foundation settling -- Georgia's red clay soil expands and contracts with moisture, causing cracks
  • Moisture intrusion -- High humidity leads to crawl space moisture, mold, and wood rot
  • HVAC wear -- Systems work overtime in Atlanta's hot summers
  • Outdated electrical -- Especially in historic neighborhoods with older wiring
  • Roof damage -- Georgia storms, hail, and fallen tree limbs take a toll

Carl knows exactly where to look for these Atlanta-specific problems.

Do you inspect new construction homes?

Yes, and we strongly recommend it. New construction does not mean problem-free. We regularly find issues in new builds across Metro Atlanta -- improperly graded lots, missing flashing, HVAC installation errors, plumbing leaks behind walls, and code violations. A builder's warranty is only as good as what you catch. Schedule a new construction inspection before your final walkthrough.

Can you inspect condos and townhomes in Atlanta?

Absolutely. We inspect condos, townhomes, single-family homes, and multi-unit properties throughout Metro Atlanta. For condos and townhomes, we focus on the interior systems, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, windows, and any areas within your unit's boundaries. We also note any visible concerns with shared structural elements. Contact us to discuss your specific property type.
