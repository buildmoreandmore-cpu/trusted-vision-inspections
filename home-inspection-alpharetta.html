Alpharetta Home Inspection Specialists

Alpharetta has evolved from a quiet North Fulton town into one of Metro Atlanta's most sought-after addresses. Anchored by the Technology Park corridor and the upscale Avalon mixed-use development, this city attracts buyers looking for excellent schools, modern amenities, and well-built homes. But even in a market dominated by newer construction, a professional home inspection is essential to protect your investment.

Carl Hall and Trusted Vision Home Inspections bring over 15 years of experience inspecting Alpharetta properties. The city's housing stock is overwhelmingly modern, with the majority of homes built between the 1990s and today. Subdivisions like Windward, Newtown, and Cambridge Park feature homes with complex rooflines, engineered systems, and builder-grade materials that require careful evaluation. We know what to look for in production-built homes -- from improperly installed flashing and inadequate attic ventilation to HVAC systems that are undersized for the square footage.

The newer construction in developments around Downtown Alpharetta, along Old Milton Parkway, and near the Avalon district includes townhomes, luxury condos, and custom-built single-family homes. These properties often feature smart home systems, spray foam insulation, and tankless water heaters -- modern components that many inspectors are not trained to evaluate. Carl stays current with continuing education and technology to ensure every system in your Alpharetta home is properly assessed.

One of the most common issues we encounter in Alpharetta homes is related to Georgia's expansive clay soils. Even homes built on engineered foundations can develop settlement cracks, sticky doors, and uneven floors when the red clay shifts during wet and dry cycles. We pay close attention to foundation indicators, grading patterns, and drainage systems to identify problems early.

We also see a surprising number of HVAC efficiency issues in Alpharetta's larger homes. Homes over 3,500 square feet frequently have zoned systems or multiple units, and improper balancing or ductwork design can lead to hot and cold spots, high utility bills, and premature equipment failure. Our inspection evaluates not just whether the system runs, but whether it performs as intended.

Whether you are purchasing your first home in a swim-tennis subdivision or investing in a luxury property along the Chattahoochee River corridor, Trusted Vision delivers same-day, photo-documented reports that give you complete clarity on your purchase.
