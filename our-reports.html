Sample Report Sections

Here is a preview of what your inspection report will look like. Every section is organized by system, with findings categorized by severity.

Section 3: Roof System

3.1 - Missing Shingles at Ridge Line Major Defect

Multiple shingles are missing or severely damaged along the ridge line on the south-facing slope. This area shows evidence of wind damage consistent with recent storm activity. Active moisture intrusion is likely during heavy rain.

Recommendation: Have a licensed roofing contractor evaluate and repair before closing. Estimated repair: $800 - $1,500.
3.2 - Gutter Downspout Discharge Minor Defect

Two gutter downspouts on the east side discharge water directly at the foundation. Downspout extensions or splash blocks should be installed to direct water at least 4 feet away from the foundation to prevent moisture issues.

Recommendation: Install downspout extensions. DIY fix, cost: $20 - $50.

Section 7: Electrical System

7.1 - Double-Tapped Breaker Safety Concern

Two circuits are connected to a single 20-amp breaker in the main electrical panel (double-tapped). This is a fire hazard because the breaker terminal is designed for only one conductor. This condition can cause loose connections, arcing, and overheating.

Recommendation: Have a licensed electrician correct before closing. Estimated repair: $150 - $300.

Section 9: HVAC System

9.1 - HVAC Filter Condition Maintenance

The HVAC air filter is heavily clogged with dust and debris. A dirty filter restricts airflow, reduces system efficiency, increases energy costs, and can shorten the lifespan of the HVAC system. Atlanta's high pollen counts make regular filter changes especially important.

Recommendation: Replace filter immediately and every 60-90 days going forward. Cost: $15 - $30.
