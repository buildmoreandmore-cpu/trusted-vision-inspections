Trusted Home Inspections in Dunwoody

Dunwoody sits at the crossroads of DeKalb and Fulton counties, making it one of the most desirable suburban communities inside the Perimeter. From the established neighborhoods surrounding Dunwoody Village to the modern townhome developments near Perimeter Mall, the housing stock here is as varied as the families who call it home. That variety is exactly why you need an inspector who knows what to look for in every type of Dunwoody property.

I'm Carl Hall, owner and lead inspector at Trusted Vision Home Inspections. I've inspected hundreds of homes across Dunwoody's zip codes -- 30338 and 30346 -- and I understand the specific challenges these properties present. Many of the single-family homes in Georgetown, Branches, and Winters Chapel were built during the 1970s and 1980s building boom. These homes often have aging HVAC systems, original polybutylene plumbing, and electrical panels that no longer meet modern safety standards. If you're buying one of these classic Dunwoody homes, my 400+ point inspection will identify every issue before you close.

On the other end of the spectrum, Dunwoody has seen a surge of new-construction townhomes and condominiums along the Perimeter Center corridor. New construction comes with its own set of concerns -- rushed framing, improperly installed flashing, HVAC systems that haven't been balanced, and grading issues that can lead to water intrusion. Just because a home is new doesn't mean it's problem-free, and my inspections hold builders accountable to the standards your investment deserves.

Dunwoody's mature tree canopy is one of its greatest assets, but large hardwoods close to rooflines create specific risks. Overhanging branches cause accelerated shingle wear, leaf accumulation in gutters leads to fascia rot, and root systems can compromise foundation integrity and underground plumbing. During every Dunwoody inspection, I pay close attention to tree-related damage that other inspectors overlook.

The red clay soil common throughout DeKalb County also plays a significant role in foundation health. Seasonal expansion and contraction of clay soil causes shifting that can produce cracks in slab foundations and stress on pier-and-beam supports. I document all evidence of movement and advise you on whether what I find is cosmetic settling or structural concern that warrants further evaluation.

Whether you're a first-time buyer looking at a starter townhome near the Dunwoody MARTA station or a growing family moving into a four-bedroom in Vermack-Clairmont, you deserve to know exactly what you're buying. I deliver same-day reports with detailed photos, clear explanations, and honest assessments so you can negotiate with confidence.
