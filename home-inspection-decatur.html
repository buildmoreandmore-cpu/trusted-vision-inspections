Decatur Home Inspection Specialists

Decatur is one of Metro Atlanta's most desirable communities, known for its walkable downtown, excellent schools, and charming older homes. From the craftsman bungalows of Oakhurst to the tree-lined streets near Agnes Scott College, Decatur offers character and community that buyers love. But these beloved older homes require an inspector who understands the unique challenges they present.

Carl Hall and Trusted Vision Home Inspections have inspected hundreds of Decatur properties over the past 15 years. The city's housing stock spans from early 1900s bungalows to mid-century ranches to contemporary infill construction. Each era brings specific concerns: original knob-and-tube wiring in attics, cast-iron drain lines reaching end of life, pier-and-beam foundations settling unevenly in Georgia's red clay, and HVAC systems retrofitted into homes that were built before air conditioning existed.

Decatur's mature tree canopy is part of what makes the city special, but large oaks and pines can cause problems that buyers should know about before closing. Root intrusion into sewer lines is common in older neighborhoods. Overhanging branches accelerate roof deterioration. And fallen limbs during storms can reveal damage that's been patched rather than properly repaired. We look carefully at the relationship between mature trees and the homes they shade.

Many Decatur homes have been renovated over the years, and not all renovation work meets current standards. We frequently find unpermitted additions, electrical work that bypasses the panel, and bathroom renovations that create moisture problems. Our inspection documents the current condition regardless of what improvements the seller claims to have made.

The neighborhoods surrounding downtown Decatur -- Oakhurst, Winnona Park, Great Lakes, and MAK Historic District -- feature homes that range from meticulously preserved to significantly modified. Whether you're purchasing a move-in-ready bungalow or a fixer-upper with original details, Trusted Vision helps you understand exactly what you're getting.

Georgia's humidity creates additional challenges for Decatur's older homes. Crawlspaces without proper vapor barriers, bathroom vents that terminate in attics, and original single-pane windows can all contribute to moisture problems that lead to mold, wood rot, and structural damage. We check every area where moisture could be causing hidden problems.
