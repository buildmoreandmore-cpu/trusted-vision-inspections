Comprehensive home inspections across Cobb County -- from historic Marietta Square to East Cobb and every neighborhood in between.

Your Marietta Home Inspection Expert

Marietta is one of Metro Atlanta's most established and diverse communities. As the county seat of Cobb County, it has been a center of Georgia life since the 1830s, and its housing stock reflects nearly two centuries of architectural evolution. Whether you're purchasing a restored Victorian near the Marietta Square, a mid-century ranch in West Side, or a sprawling executive home in East Cobb, the age and style of the property directly shapes the inspection concerns you need to understand.

I'm Carl Hall, and I've built Trusted Vision Home Inspections on one principle: every buyer deserves to see their investment clearly before they sign. Marietta's breadth of housing types means no two inspections are alike, and that's exactly why experience matters. I've personally completed more than 2,500 inspections across Metro Atlanta, with Cobb County being one of my most active service areas.

Homes built in the 1950s and 1960s around the Whitlock Avenue and Powder Springs corridors often feature original cast-iron drain lines, outdated electrical panels like Federal Pacific or Zinsco brands, and single-pane windows that drive up energy costs. In the historic district near the Square, you'll find charming craftsman and Victorian-era properties that may carry knob-and-tube wiring, deteriorated pier-and-beam foundations, or lead-based paint beneath layers of more recent finishes. My inspection covers all of these concerns with a thorough, systematic approach.

East Cobb represents the newer end of Marietta's housing market, with subdivisions built from the 1980s through the 2000s. These properties often appear move-in ready on the surface, but common issues include failing composition shingle roofs approaching the end of their 20-25 year lifespan, builder-grade HVAC systems that were undersized from the start, and improper grading that directs stormwater toward the foundation rather than away from it. I identify these issues before they become your financial burden.

Marietta's geography also introduces unique inspection considerations. The rolling terrain of Cobb County means many homes are built on sloped lots, which increases the risk of drainage problems, retaining wall failures, and crawl space moisture intrusion. The red clay subsoil retains water and expands when saturated, putting lateral pressure on basement walls and contributing to slab cracks. My inspections include careful evaluation of all moisture-related concerns, including crawl space conditions, gutter performance, and exterior grading.

Every inspection I complete comes with a detailed same-day report featuring annotated photographs and clear, plain-language descriptions of every finding. I don't just list problems -- I explain what they mean, how urgent they are, and what it might cost to address them. That gives you the information you need to negotiate effectively or walk away with confidence.
